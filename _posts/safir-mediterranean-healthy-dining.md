## Is Mediterranean Food Always Healthy, Especially at a Restaurant Like Safir?

Many people rightly associate the Mediterranean diet with incredible health benefits, like a lower risk of cardiovascular diseases and improved longevity (1). However, the restaurant reality can sometimes differ from the traditional principles. While home-cooked Mediterranean meals emphasize fresh, whole ingredients, restaurant versions, even at a beloved spot like Safir, can introduce hidden pitfalls.

Portion sizes tend to be much larger than what you’d eat at home, and cooking methods might involve more oil – sometimes less healthy seed oils – and higher sodium content. Dressings can be calorie-dense, and even seemingly healthy items like falafel, while plant-based, are often deep-fried, absorbing considerable oil.

## What Are the Best Choices on a Mediterranean Menu for My Health Goals?

Making smart choices starts with understanding what to look for. Prioritize lean proteins like grilled chicken (like shish tawook) or fish. Load up on plenty of vegetables, whether in salads (dressing on the side, please!) or as grilled sides. Whole grains, such as bulgur, are great in moderation.

For example, opting for baba ghanoush over hummus can sometimes save on calories. When considering a shawarma plate, ask for extra vegetables and less rice, or choose a kebab with grilled veggies. ## How Can I Spot Hidden Ingredients and Make Smarter Swaps at Safir?

Being an informed diner means asking a few questions and being ready to request modifications. Don't hesitate to inquire about the type of cooking oil used or whether dressings can be served on the side. Grilled options are generally better than fried ones. For example, if your meal comes with fries, ask if you can swap them for grilled vegetables or a side salad.

![a plate of grilled chicken with vegetables and a plate of fried falafel with rice](/assets/images/blog-article/safir-mediterranean-healthy-dining-1.jpg)

Even small changes can make a big difference. Requesting less pita bread with your hummus or choosing a lentil soup (often packed with fiber and nutrients) as an appetizer can help you manage your overall intake without sacrificing flavor.
