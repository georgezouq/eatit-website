## Are You Accidentally Undermining Your Diet? The Surprising Truth About What We Consume.

It might sound strange, but something you *inhale* could be impacting your health goals. A common misconception is that vaping is calorie-neutral, meaning it has no caloric impact on your body. This belief often stems from the idea that since you're not eating it, it can't contribute to your daily calorie count. However, the reality is a bit more nuanced than that.

## What Exactly Are Vapes Made Of, Anyway? Deconstructing E-Liquids

To understand the caloric implications of vaping, we first need to look at what e-liquids are made of. The primary components of most vape juices are Propylene Glycol (PG) and Vegetable Glycerin (VG), along with flavorings and, often, nicotine.

Both PG and VG are types of alcohol, and like other ingestible alcohols (think ethanol in alcoholic beverages), they contain caloric energy. They serve as the base for the e-liquid, carrying the flavors and nicotine, and producing the vapor when heated. While flavorings typically contribute minimal calories, the bulk of any caloric content comes from these two main ingredients.

## So, How Many Calories Are Actually in That Vape Puff? Unpacking the Numbers

This is where the direct answer to "how many calories are in a vape" comes in. Research indicates that both Propylene Glycol (PG) and Vegetable Glycerin (VG) contain approximately 4.3 calories per gram. While the metabolism of inhaled substances isn't identical to digested food, your body does process these compounds, converting them into energy.

Consider an average daily e-liquid consumption. For regular vapers, this can range from 2 to 5 milliliters (mL) per day. Since VG and PG have densities similar to water (roughly 1 gram per mL), this means a few grams of these substances could be inhaled daily. Even if absorption isn't 100%, and the exact energy conversion varies among individuals, these are not zero-calorie substances. Over time, these small, consistent "hidden" calories can certainly add up. If you're consuming 5mL of e-liquid daily, and assuming moderate absorption, you could be looking at an additional 20-40 calories or more per day from PG and VG alone. While this might seem small, it's a factor that can impact weight management when every calorie counts for your specific goals.

## Is Vaping Making You Gain Weight? Beyond Just Calories

The direct caloric impact from vaping might be modest compared to a full meal, but its consistent, daily nature means it can contribute to an overall caloric surplus over weeks and months. Even small, consistent hidden calories can accumulate and make it harder to reach weight loss goals or easier to gain weight unintentionally. Think of it like a few extra bites of a snack each day – individually insignificant, but collectively impactful.
