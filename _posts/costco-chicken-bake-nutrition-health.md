**Quick answer:** The Costco Chicken Bake is a high-calorie, high-sodium comfort food. Estimates often place it around 770 calories with high fat and sodium, so it’s best treated as an occasional meal.

The Chicken Bake is a baked pastry filled with chicken, bacon, cheese, and dressing. Figures vary by location and recipe, so check current nutrition info when available.

## Estimated nutrition highlights (varies by location)

| Nutrient | Commonly reported estimate | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Calories | Around 770 | A large share of daily intake. |
| Fat | Around 46g | Mostly from cheese, bacon, and dressing. |
| Sodium | Often near 2,300 mg | Can approach a full day’s limit. |
| Carbs | Around 56g | Mainly from the crust. |
| Protein | Around 42g | Solid protein, but paired with high fat. |

## How it fits different goals

| Goal | What to watch | Simple approach |

- Share it or save half for later.
- Balance the rest of the day with vegetables and lean protein.
- Log it so weekly trends stay visible.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is the Chicken Bake okay once in a while?

Yes. Most people treat it as an occasional indulgence rather than a regular meal.

### Why is the sodium so high?

Processed meats, cheese, and sauces add up quickly.

### Can I make it fit my macros?

It depends on your day. Logging it and adjusting other meals is the simplest approach.

### What’s a lighter alternative at the food court?
