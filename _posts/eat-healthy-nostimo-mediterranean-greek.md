## Is Every Dish at Nostimo Truly "Healthy" Just Because it's Greek?

While the Mediterranean diet is celebrated worldwide for its incredible health benefits, it's a bit of a myth that every single dish on a Greek restaurant menu is automatically a picture of health. Many dishes, while delicious, can be calorie-dense, especially when prepared in commercial kitchens. Take moussaka, for instance; with its rich layers of béchamel sauce, meat, and potatoes, it can be quite a hearty meal. Similarly, spanakopita, made with flaky phyllo dough and cheese, packs a significant calorie punch. Even a seemingly innocent Greek salad can become a calorie bomb if it's swimming in dressing and loaded with excessive feta and olives. Restaurants often prepare dishes with larger portions and more oil than you might use at home, which can quickly add up.

## How Can I Pick the Best Options for My Diet at Nostimo?

Navigating the menu at Nostimo doesn't have to be a guessing game. A few simple strategies can help you make delicious and smart choices. Focus on grilled proteins like souvlaki (ask for chicken or lean pork) or grilled fish, which are excellent sources of lean protein without excessive fat. Prioritize vegetable-rich dishes; opt for salads with the dressing on the side so you can control the amount, or try traditional "horta" (boiled greens) if available.

Be mindful of portion sizes, especially with appetizers and main courses, and don't be afraid to share. When possible, ask about cooking methods – baked or grilled is generally preferable to fried. You might also want to inquire about the type of oil used; while traditional Greek cooking champions olive oil, some restaurants might use cheaper seed oils for cost-effectiveness, which can alter the health profile of your meal.

## What About Hidden Allergens or Unwanted Ingredients in Greek Cuisine?

For those with dietary restrictions or allergies, Greek cuisine, while wonderfully diverse, does contain several common culprits. Dairy, found in feta cheese, yogurt (a staple in tzatziki), and various creamy sauces, is prevalent. Gluten is often present in pita bread, pastries like spanakopita and baklava, and occasionally as a thickening agent in sauces. Nuts, particularly walnuts and pistachios, are frequent guests in desserts like baklava. Seafood, a healthy choice for many, is a major allergen for others. And let’s not forget sesame, often found in tahini-based dips.

Beyond allergens, many people are increasingly concerned about the prevalence of seed oils in restaurant cooking. While traditional Mediterranean cooking is built around olive oil, some modern restaurants may use oils like canola, soybean, or sunflower oil due to cost or higher smoke points. It's often hard to tell without asking.
