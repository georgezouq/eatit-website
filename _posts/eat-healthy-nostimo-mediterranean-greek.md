**Quick answer:** Yes. At Nostimo, grilled proteins, salads, and vegetable-forward plates make it easier to stay within common nutrition goals.

Nostimo Mediterranean Greek Restaurant serves classic Greek dishes that often include olive oil, seafood, and fresh vegetables. With portion control and a few swaps, it can fit many health plans.

![A collage of Greek dishes, highlighting a rich moussaka and a heavily dressed Greek salad](/assets/images/blog-article/eat-healthy-nostimo-mediterranean-greek-1.jpg)

## What to order for a lighter meal

| Goal | Better choice | Why it works |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Lower sugar | Grilled protein + salad | Fewer hidden sugars. |
| Lower sodium | Sauce on the side | Reduces salt load. |
| Seed-oil-free | Ask about cooking oils | Confirms preparation. |
| Higher protein | Souvlaki or grilled fish | Leaner main dish. |

![An infographic highlighting common allergens like dairy, gluten, nuts, and seafood in typical Greek ingredients](/assets/images/blog-article/eat-healthy-nostimo-mediterranean-greek-2.jpg)

## Common ingredients to watch

- Dairy in tzatziki and feta.
- Gluten in pita and pastries.
- Nuts in desserts like baklava.
- Seafood if you have allergies.

## Frequently asked questions

### Are all Greek dishes healthy?

Not necessarily. Fried foods and heavy sauces can add calories quickly.

### How can I keep portions reasonable?

Share an appetizer and focus on one main protein and one side.

### Can I keep it gluten-free?

Yes. Choose grilled dishes without pita or pastries.

### Should I log restaurant meals?

Yes. Logging helps you keep weekly trends on track.
