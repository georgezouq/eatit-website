## How Many Calories Are Really in a Fuji Apple?

A medium Fuji apple (approximately 182 grams) generally contains around 96 calories with the skin on. This makes it a relatively low-calorie, high-fiber choice for a snack. The calorie count can vary slightly depending on the exact size; a small Fuji might have closer to 70-80 calories, while a larger one could be upwards of 110-120 calories. Keeping the skin on is key, as it provides a significant portion of the fiber and nutrients.

## What Else Does a Fuji Apple Bring to the Table? (Beyond Just Calories)

Think of it as more than just energy; it's a little package of goodness. Beyond calories, Fuji apples offer a robust nutritional profile. Per 100 grams, they primarily consist of carbohydrates (around 13.8g), with a healthy portion of that being dietary fiber (2.4g) and natural sugars (10.39g). They contain negligible amounts of protein (0.26g) and fat (0.17g). Fuji apples are also a good source of Vitamin C and various antioxidants, contributing to overall health beyond just caloric intake.

## Is a Fuji Apple a "Good" Choice for My Diet?

The "good" or "bad" label often depends entirely on *your* unique health journey. For most people, a Fuji apple is an excellent choice for a snack or part of a meal. Its fiber content helps with satiety, which can be beneficial for weight management, and the natural sugars provide a quick, sustained energy boost without the crash associated with processed sweets.

Compared to other popular varieties, Fuji apples have a similar caloric content to Gala apples (around 57 calories per 100g for Gala, versus 52 calories per 100g for Fuji), while Granny Smith apples are slightly lower in calories (around 47 calories per 100g). Its sweetness makes it a satisfying choice for those looking to reduce added sugars in their diet. Whole fruits like apples are recommended across most dietary guidelines due to their nutrient density and positive impact on health.
